# The Crimson Sage
*The Wandering Scholar, Keeper of Forgotten Truths*

## Overview
The Crimson Sage appears as a mysterious scholar researching crystal corruption and ancient history. In truth, they are Aetheron, the shattered God-King whose crown became the Aether Crystals. Stripped of most divine power and trapped in mortal form, they seek to understand what they have become and perhaps find redemption—or revenge.

## Appearance
- **Visible**: Elderly human, flowing crimson robes, silver hair
- **Height**: 5'10" (variable)
- **Eyes**: Change color based on mood (gold when using power)
- **Staff**: Ancient wood wrapped with crystal veins
- **True Form**: Glimpses of divine radiance, countless fractal reflections

## Personality
- **Surface**: Wise, cryptic, helpful but mysterious
- **Deep**: Tormented by loss, wrestling with godhood memories
- **Motivations**: Complex and contradictory
- **Speech**: Speaks in metaphors and half-truths

## The Truth
> [!warning] Ultimate Secret - DM Only
> The Crimson Sage is Aetheron, the God-King whose crown shattered 500 years ago. When the Seven Advisors betrayed him, the shattering scattered his essence across the crystals. He reformed as this diminished mortal form, retaining knowledge but not omnipotence. He both created and is victim of the crystal corruption.

### Legendary Abilities
**Divine Essence.** The Crimson Sage is the shattered god Aetheron. Cannot be truly killed - reforms in 1d10 days if destroyed.

**Shard Sense.** Knows the exact location and condition of all Seven Shards at all times. Can teleport to any Shard instantly.

**Reality Manipulation.** Can alter local reality as a legendary action. Change terrain, weather, or physical laws within 1 mile.

**Perfect Memory.** Remembers every moment since the Shattering 500 years ago. Cannot be deceived about historical events.

**Spellcasting.** 20th-level spellcaster. Spell save DC 22, +14 spell attack. Can cast any spell from any class list. Spell slots: 4/3/3/3/3/2/2/2/1/1.

### Divine Actions
**Multiattack.** The Sage makes three attacks or casts two spells.

**Staff of Shattered Divinity.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +8 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 12 (2d8 + 2) bludgeoning damage plus 21 (6d6) force damage. Can cast any spell through the staff.

**Divine Word.** Target within 120 feet must make DC 22 Wisdom save. On failure: deafened, blinded, stunned for 1 minute. If they have fewer than 100 HP, they are banished to another plane.

**Shard Recall (1/Day).** Instantly summon any of the Seven Shards to his location, regardless of distance or obstacles.

**Temporal Glimpse.** Show target a vision of their past, present, or potential future. Can be used to provide information or inflict trauma (8d6 psychic damage, DC 22 Wisdom save for half).

### Legendary Actions (3 per turn)
- Move without provoking opportunity attacks
- Cast a spell of 3rd level or lower

- Alter local reality in minor way (costs 2 actions)

## The Transformation Process
### From God-King to Mortal
The transformation from Aetheron the God-King to the Crimson Sage was neither instant nor complete. It occurred over decades as his shattered essence slowly coalesced into this diminished form.

### Physical Changes
- **Initial Form**: Raw divine energy scattered across the crystals
- **First Manifestation**: Ghostly apparition with fluctuating appearance
- **Stabilization**: Gradual solidification into the current human-like form
- **Ongoing Evolution**: Still changing subtly as he reconnects with his scattered power

### Mental Reconstruction
- **Fragmented Memories**: Initially could only remember pieces of his divine existence
- **Personality Integration**: Had to rebuild his sense of self from scattered thoughts
- **Emotional Development**: Learning to feel human emotions for the first time
- **Moral Complexity**: Developing new perspectives on good, evil, and morality

## The Seven Advisors - The Betrayers Revealed
### The Original Conspiracy
The seven advisors who shattered Aetheron's crown were not acting from pure malice, but from genuine belief that his godhood had become corrupted and dangerous.

### The Betrayers' Names and Fates
1. **Marcus the Just** - Ancestor of [[02_Worldbuilding/People/High Inquisitor Maltheos|02 Worldbuilding/People/High Inquisitor Maltheos]]
2. **Seraphina the Wise** - Founded the first crystal healing academy
3. **Gareth Ironheart** - Military commander who died in the Shattering
4. **Lysander the Scholar** - Created the first crystal research protocols
5. **Nerissa Tidecaller** - Ancestor of several current noble families
6. **Valdris the Merchant** - Ancestor of [[02_Worldbuilding/People/Lord Aurelius Goldwave|02 Worldbuilding/People/Lord Aurelius Goldwave]]
7. **Thrace the Builder** - Architect of the original crystal containment systems

### Their Justification
- Aetheron's divine power was becoming unstable and dangerous
- The crown was amplifying his worst impulses and paranoid tendencies
- They believed shattering the crown would free him to rule more wisely
- They never intended to create the crystal corruption crisis
- Each carried guilt about the consequences until their deaths

## The Crimson Sage's Secret Plans
### Multiple Concurrent Agendas
The Sage operates several overlapping schemes simultaneously, some contradicting others as he remains genuinely uncertain about his ultimate goals.

### Plan A: Restoration
- Gather all Seven Shards to restore his godhood
- Use divine power to heal the crystal corruption
- Resume rule over a united and prosperous kingdom
- Risk: Power could corrupt him again

### Plan B: Redemption
- Destroy all Shards including himself to end corruption forever
- Ensure the Seven Advisors' descendants can rule wisely
- Accept oblivion as atonement for his failures
- Risk: Leaves kingdom vulnerable to other threats

### Plan C: Evolution
- Create a new form of divinity shared among multiple worthy individuals
- Transform the Shards into tools for collective rather than individual power
- Establish a new system of governance beyond monarchy or democracy
- Risk: Unknown consequences of unprecedented magical experiment

### Plan D: Guidance
- Remain as advisor and teacher to mortal rulers
- Use his knowledge and power to subtly guide civilization
- Never reclaim full godhood but never fully give up divine nature
- Risk: Immortal meddling could create dependency or resentment

## Final Secrets for the Dungeon Master
### The Ultimate Truth About the Shattering
The Seven Advisors didn't betray Aetheron - they saved the world from him. His divine consciousness had been corrupted by an entity from beyond the material plane, and the crown's shattering was the only way to break that influence. The Crimson Sage is slowly remembering this truth and must decide whether to reclaim godhood or accept that his advisors were heroes.

### The Real Purpose of Crystal Corruption
The corruption isn't a side effect of the Shattering - it's Aetheron's divine essence trying to reunite with living beings. Every corrupted individual carries a fragment of his consciousness, and curing them means destroying part of his own soul. This is why no permanent cure has been found - it would require the Sage to sacrifice his immortality and memories.

### The Coming Choice
The campaign will eventually force the Sage to choose between four ultimate fates:
1. **Restoration:** Reclaim godhood but risk corruption returning
2. **Sacrifice:** Destroy himself to end corruption forever
3. **Evolution:** Transform into something new and unprecedented
4. **Acceptance:** Remain as guide and protector in diminished form

This choice will determine not just his fate, but the future of magic, monarchy, and mortality itself.
