# CANONICAL_WORLD_MAP.md
*Definitive Geographic Reference for the Aethermoor-Aquabyssos Dual Realm*

## Overview
This document establishes the canonical geography for the interconnected realms of Aethermoor (surface/sky realm) and Aquabyssos (underwater realm). Both realms exist in dimensional overlap, with corresponding locations that can phase between surface and depth states.

## Primary Realms

### Aethermoor (Surface/Sky Realm)
**Type**: Crystalline continent with magical sky-cities
**Primary Elevation**: Sea level to 15,000 feet above
**Climate**: Temperate with magical weather patterns

### Aquabyssos (Depth Realm) 
**Type**: Underwater civilization
**Primary Depth Range**: Surface to 12,000 feet below sea level
**Environment**: High-pressure underwater with magical air pockets

## Continental Structure

### Major Landmasses/Depth Zones

#### Central Continent - Aethermoor Prime / Aquabyssos Prime
**Aethermoor Surface Form**:
- **Capital**: Crystalhaven (Pop: ~45,000)
- **Major Cities**: Port Meridian (Pop: ~85,000), Lumengarde (Pop: ~45,000)
- **Terrain**: Crystal-infused lands, floating crystal formations
- **Dimensions**: ~800 miles north-south, ~600 miles east-west

**Aquabyssos Depth Form**:
- **Capital**: Abyssos Prime (Pop: 2,000,000 - Depth: 1,000-3,000 feet)
- **Major Cities**: Port Meridian Harbor District (Depth: 847 feet, Pop: ~15,000)
- **Terrain**: Underwater cities in pressure-resistant domes
- **Pressure Zones**: Varies from 26 atmospheres to 90+ atmospheres

#### Northern Territories
**Aethermoor**: The Crystalline Wastes
- **Type**: Uncontrolled crystal growth zones
- **Elevation**: 500-8,000 feet above sea level
- **Hazards**: Wild magic, crystal corruption

**Aquabyssos**: The Hadal Depths
- **Type**: Extreme depth zones 
- **Depth**: 8,000-12,000+ feet
- **Pressure**: 240-360+ atmospheres
- **Population**: Unknown (Non-Euclidean entities)

#### Southern Territories  
**Aethermoor**: The Verdant Reach
- **Type**: Forests resisting crystal expansion
- **Major City**: Sylvanheart (Pop: 180,000)
- **Elevation**: Sea level to 2,000 feet

**Aquabyssos**: Corallion
- **Type**: Living coral cities
- **Depth**: 200-2,000 feet
- **Population**: ~500,000 (integrated with coral ecosystem)

#### Eastern Regions
**Aethermoor**: The Sundered Peaks
- **Type**: Mountain range, crystal sources
- **Major City**: Ironhold (Pop: 280,000)  
- **Elevation**: 3,000-15,000 feet

**Aquabyssos**: Crystallium
- **Type**: Underwater crystal formations
- **Depth**: 4,000-8,000 feet

#### Western Regions
**Aethermoor**: The Azure Coast
- **Type**: Trading ports and naval bases
- **Major City**: Port Meridian (Harbor)
- **Population**: 850,000 (coastal settlements)

**Aquabyssos**: The Pressure Depths
- **Type**: Commercial and transport hubs
- **Depth**: Variable (500-5,000 feet)

## Transportation Networks

### Depth-Based Transit (Aquabyssos)
1. **Pressure Tubes**: Rapid transit between depth stations
   - Primary Route: Abyssos Prime ↔ Port Meridian Harbor (847 ft → 2,000 ft)
   - Secondary Routes: Major cities at 1,000+ foot intervals

2. **Current Highways**: Natural and artificial current systems
   - The Kelp Highway: Major commercial route
   - Blood Current Express: Through leviathan circulatory systems
   - Void Currents: Dangerous but fast deep-sea routes

3. **Memory Streams**: Consciousness-only travel
   - Connect major knowledge centers
   - Limited duration (1d4 hours)

### Altitude-Based Transit (Aethermoor)
1. **Crystal Flight Paths**: Magical flight routes between sky-cities
   - Maintained by resonance towers
   - Elevation: 1,000-10,000 feet

2. **Mountain Passes**: Traditional overland routes
   - The Iron Road: Crystalhaven ↔ Ironhold
   - Coastal Highway: Port Meridian coastal network

3. **Airship Networks**: Crystal-powered aerial transport
   - Major hubs at 5,000+ feet elevation
   - Weather-dependent schedules

### Cross-Realm Conduits
1. **Lighthouse Network**: Primary dimensional bridges
   - Lighthouse of Storms: Main portal (Variable depth/altitude)
   - Seven lesser lighthouses: Regional access points

2. **Deep Wells**: Ancient vertical shafts
   - Connect surface cities to depth equivalents
   - Require advanced pressure adaptation

3. **Shadow Passages**: Unstable dimensional rifts
   - Access through shadow separation
   - High risk of dimensional displacement

## Resource Distribution

### Aethermoor Resources
- **Aether Crystals**: Primary magical resource
  - Clear: Versatile energy (Found: All elevations)
  - Colored: Specialized magic (Found: Specific elevation bands)
  - Gold: Divine essence (Found: 10,000+ feet only)

- **Sky Metals**: Lighter-than-air metallurgy
- **Wind Essence**: Atmospheric magic capture
- **Mountain Minerals**: Traditional mining (Iron, copper, gems)

### Aquabyssos Resources  
- **Pressure Pearls**: Crystallized emotion/experience currency
- **Memory Crystals**: Stored consciousness fragments
- **Depth Minerals**: Pressure-formed rare elements
- **Bio-Luminescent Substances**: Living light sources
- **Leviathan Products**: From deep-sea massive creatures

### Shared/Contested Resources
- **Temporal Fragments**: Time-distortion materials
- **Shadow Essence**: Dimensional energy
- **Reality Anchors**: Stability-maintaining objects
- **Convergence Crystals**: Enable realm-crossing

## Climate and Environmental Patterns

### Aethermoor Weather Systems
- **Crystal Storms**: Magical weather affecting 5,000+ feet
- **Resonance Winds**: Harmonic atmospheric currents  
- **Elevation Effects**: Temperature drops 3°F per 1,000 feet
- **Seasons**: Traditional four-season cycle

### Aquabyssos Pressure Systems
- **Depth Seasons**: Based on current patterns, not solar
  - Deep Current Season: Cold water from below
  - Thermal Bloom Season: Volcanic warming
  - Abyssal Summer: Stable pressure zones
  - Crushing Depths: Unstable pressure variance

- **Pressure Gradient**: +3 atmospheres per 100 feet depth
- **Memory Tides**: Twice monthly psychological weather

### Cross-Realm Phenomena
- **Reality Storms**: Dimensional instability weather
- **Convergence Zones**: Areas of overlapping weather
- **Temporal Eddies**: Time-differential weather patterns
