### Writing Tips

#### For Players Writing Recaps

**Keep It Engaging:**
- Write from your character's perspective
- Include emotional reactions and thoughts
- Highlight what was important to your character
- Don't just list events - explain why they mattered

**Include Everyone:**
- Give each character at least one moment
- Mention cool things other players did
- Note character interactions and relationships
- Share the spotlight in your writing

**Be Helpful:**
- Include important plot information
- Note consequences of party decisions
- Track ongoing storylines and mysteries
- Remember details that might be forgotten

#### For GMs Using Player Recaps

**Encourage Participation:**
- Rotate who writes the recap
- Offer small in-game rewards for good recaps
- Use player perspectives to improve your game
- Incorporate player observations into future sessions

**Use as Feedback:**
- See what players remember most
- Understand what resonated emotionally
- Identify missed opportunities
- Track which storylines are most engaging

### Different Recap Styles

**In-Character Journal:** Written as character's personal notes
**Dramatic Narrative:** Story-style writing with dialogue and description
**Bullet Point Summary:** Quick, efficient list of events and outcomes
**Collaborative Doc:** Multiple players contributing different sections
**Video/Audio:** Recorded discussion of session highlights

### Sample Formats

#### Quick Format (15 minutes)
- What happened?
- Who did what?
- What did we learn?
- What's next?

#### Standard Format (30 minutes)
- Session overview
- Key events
- Character moments
- Consequences
- Next steps

#### Detailed Format (60+ minutes)
- Full narrative recap
- Character development analysis
- Plot thread tracking
- NPC relationship updates
- World state changes
