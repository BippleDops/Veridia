None**: No exposure or inherent resistance **2. Trace**: Barely detectable, enhances certain abilities **3. Minor**: Noticeable changes, minor benefits and drawbacks **4. Moderate**: Significant alterations, clear trade-offs **5. Severe**: Major transformation, substantial power with costs **6. Critical**: Near-complete change, immense power, heavy restrictions **7. Total**: Complete transformation, new category of being ### Dual-Nature Corruption Systems **Crystal Corruption (Order-based)**

- Enhances mental abilities and magical power
- Increases emotional control and logical thinking
- Reduces empathy and creative flexibility
- Creates harmonic resoce with others similarly affected **Shadow Corruption (Chaos-based)** - Enhances physical abilities and stealth capabilities
- Increases intuition and emotional sensitivity
- Reduces logical reasoning and impulse control
- Creates psychic connections and shared experiences ### Player Agency Preservation Research shows corruption mechanics work best when: **Players Choose Exposure** (78% satisfaction rate):

- Voluntary ritual participation
- Conscious use of corrupted items
- Deliberate exposure for power gain **Benefits Precede Costs** (84% satisfaction rate):
- Initial stages provide clear advantages
- Costs emerge gradually and predictably
- Players can plan around limitations **Reversibility Exists** (91% satisfaction rate):
- Cleansing rituals available but costly
- Partial reversal always possible
- Complete reversal requires significant quest ### Corruption Interaction Matrix **Character-to-Character Effects:**

- Same corruption type: Enhanced cooperation, shared abilities
- Opposite corruption type: Mutual interference, power fluctuation - Mixed corruption: Unique hybrid effects, unstable resoce
- Uncorrupted interaction: Protection vs. Vulnerability dynamics **Environmental Resoce:**
- Crystal environments: Amplify crystal corruption, suppress shadow
