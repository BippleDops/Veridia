## Physical Description

The Shadow Warren's true appearance is a mystery, as they are never seen without their distinctive shadow-wreathed cloak that seems to shift and move independently. What can be discerned suggests a being profoundly altered by shadow corruption:

**Height**: Approximately 5'8", though their form seems to fluctuate
**Build**: Lean and wiry, with unnaturally elongated limbs
**Face**: Always hidden beneath a deep hood, with only occasional glimpses of pale, almost translucent skin
**Eyes**: Twin points of dim silver light that pierce through the darkness of their hood
**Voice**: A whisper that seems to come from multiple directions at once, with an echoing quality
**Distinguishing Features**:
- Shadow tendrils that extend from their cloak, acting as extra limbs
- Footsteps that make no sound, even on the loudest surfaces
- A presence that causes nearby shadows to deepen and stretch toward them
- Scars of crystalline corruption visible on their hands - the only part of their body regularly seen

- **When Pleased**: Shows open joy
- **In Conflict**: Tends toward negotiation

### Ideals and Motivations
**Primary Goal**: Overthrow the crystal-corrupted nobility of Aethermoor and establish a government that serves all citizens, not just the elite.

**Core Beliefs**:
- Power corrupts, but corruption can be turned against itself
- The common people deserve protection from both crystal and shadow corruption
- Information is the most valuable currency in any revolution
- Sometimes you must embrace darkness to bring about the light

**Fears**:
- Complete shadow corruption that would strip away their remaining humanity
- The underground network being exposed and destroyed
- Becoming the very thing they fight against
- The possibility that their efforts are ultimately futile

### Early Life
The Shadow Warren's original identity is lost to time and corruption. What is known comes from fragmented memories and careful investigation:
- Born in the lower districts of Aethermoor during the early crystal corruption period
- Lost family to crystal plague when they were young
- Survived on the streets by learning to navigate both crystal and shadow corruption zones
- Demonstrated early aptitude for shadow magic and stealth

### The Transformation
Approximately ten years ago, the individual who would become the Shadow Warren underwent a profound transformation:
- Discovered a cache of shadow corruption artifacts in the ruins beneath Aethermoor
- Made a deliberate pact with shadow entities to gain power to fight the crystal nobility
- The corruption changed their physical form but left their mind largely intact
- Emerged from months of isolation as a new being with enhanced abilities

### Rise to Leadership
Over the past decade, the Shadow Warren has built the most effective resistance network in Aethermoor:
- Started by providing safe passage through corrupted zones for refugees
- Gradually recruited other corruption survivors and disillusioned citizens
- Established the Undergrowth as a base of operations
- Created a complex information network that rivals official intelligence services
- Orchestrated several successful strikes against corrupt crystal nobles

### Recent Activities
In the past year, the Shadow Warren's activities have intensified:
- Increased recruitment efforts among both surface and underground populations
- Established contact with external resistance groups, possibly including Aquabyssos agents
- Begun planning what they call "The Great Unveiling" - a major operation to expose noble corruption
- Started investigating connections between the Seven Shards and Aethermoor's ruling class

- Employs extensive resources to hunt down and eliminate resistance cells

**The Purification Council**
- Religious zealots who believe both shadow and crystal corruption must be cleansed
- View Warren as an abomination that must be destroyed regardless of their goals

### Personal Secrets
**True Identity**: Warren was once Marcus Brightwater, a young city clerk who witnessed his family's execution by crystal guards for "corruption contamination."

**The Shadow Pact**: Warren's patron is not a typical shadow entity but a fragment of one of the Seven Shards that achieved consciousness and rebels against its corruption.

**Physical Condition**: The shadow corruption is slowly consuming Warren's life force. They have perhaps two years before complete dissolution unless they find a way to stabilize their condition.

**Hidden Agenda**: Beyond revolution, Warren seeks to find a way to purify corruption entirely - both shadow and crystal - believing this is the only way to truly save Aethermoor.

### Network Secrets
**The True Base**: The Undergrowth is just the visible layer. The real network headquarters exists in a pocket dimension accessible only through shadow magic.

**Noble Infiltration**: Several actual nobles are secretly part of the network, having been replaced by magically disguised operatives.

**The Corruption Map**: Warren possesses detailed maps of corruption spread patterns that could predict future outbreak locations.

**External Backing**: The network receives mysterious funding and resources from an unknown external source, possibly connected to the Seven Shards themselves.

### Special Abilities

**Shadow Step**: As a bonus action, Warren can teleport up to 120 feet to an unoccupied space within dim light or darkness.

**Umbral Form**: Warren can become incorporeal for up to 10 minutes per day (can be split into smaller durations). While incorporeal, they can move through objects and creatures as if they were difficult terrain.

**Corruption Aura**: Creatures within 10 feet of Warren must make a **DC 19** Wisdom saving throw at the start of their turn or be frightened until the start of their next turn.

**Spellcasting**: Warren is a 14th-level spellcaster. Their spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save **DC 19**, +11 to hit with spell attacks). Warren knows the following warlock spells:

- **Cantrips (at will)**: Eldritch blast, mage hand, minor illusion, prestidigitation
- **1st-5th level (3 5th-level slots)**: hex, darkness, invisibility, suggestion, counterspell, fly, greater invisibility, dominate person, scrying
- **Mystic Arcanum (1/day each)**: mass suggestion (6th), plane shift (7th)

### Actions

**Multiattack**: Warren makes three attacks: two with their Corruption Blade and one Eldritch Blast.

**Corruption Blade**: *Melee Weapon Attack*: +11 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit*: 9 (`dice: 1d8` + 5) slashing damage plus 7 (`dice: 2d6`) necrotic damage. This attack ignores armor and shields.

**Eldritch Blast**: *Ranged Spell Attack*: +11 to hit, range 120 ft., one target. *Hit*: 12 (`dice: 1d10` + 7) force damage. If the target is Large or smaller, it must make a **DC 19** Strength saving throw or be pushed 10 feet away from Warren.

**Shadow Bind (Recharge 5-6)**: Warren targets up to three creatures within 60 feet. Each target must make a **DC 19** Dexterity saving throw or be restrained by shadow tendrils for 1 minute. A restrained creature can use its action to make a **DC 19** Strength check, freeing itself on a success.

### Legendary Actions (3 per turn)
**Move**: Warren moves up to their speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
**Attack**: Warren makes one Corruption Blade attack or casts a cantrip.
**Shadow Step**: Warren uses their Shadow Step ability.
**Cast Spell (Costs 2 Actions)**: Warren casts a spell of 3rd level or lower.

*The Shadow Warren represents the complex moral landscape of Aethermoor, where the line between hero and villain is as murky as the shadows they inhabit. Their story serves as a powerful reminder that good intentions and noble goals can lead down dark paths, and that sometimes the price of revolution is higher than anyone anticipates.*
